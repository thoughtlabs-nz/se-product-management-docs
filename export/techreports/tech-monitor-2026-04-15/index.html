    AI & Machine Learning

    ​
    OpenAI Launches GPT-5.4-Cyber

    OpenAI unveiled GPT-5.4-Cyber, a specialized variant fine-tuned for defensive cybersecurity workflows. Features include binary reverse engineering, vulnerability scanning, and malware analysis with fewer restrictions for vetted security professionals. The company is scaling its Trusted Access for Cyber (TAC) programme and classified the model as “High” cyber capability under its Preparedness Framework. Source: Reuters, Cybersecurity News

    Anthropic’s Claude Code Updates

    Anthropic released automated “routines” and a desktop makeover for Claude Code, enhancing developer productivity tools. Source: SiliconANGLE

    Microsoft MAI-Image-2-Efficient

    Microsoft released MAI-Image-2-Efficient, described as a “production workhorse” for businesses - a faster and cheaper version claiming 41% lower cost for product shots, marketing creatives, and batch pipelines. Source: The Verge

    Nvidia Ising AI Models for Quantum Computing

    Nvidia announced Ising, the world’s first open AI model family for quantum computing calibration and error correction. Two models: Ising Decoding for real-time error correction and Ising Calibration for system calibration. Source: SiliconANGLE

    Meta and Broadcom Custom AI Processors

    Meta doubled down on partnership with Broadcom to develop custom AI processors. Source: SiliconANGLE

    Waymo Testing in London

    Waymo began testing autonomous vehicles on public roads in London, preparing for commercial robotaxi service launch in 2026. Fleet of ~100 Jaguar I-Pace vehicles with safety operators across 100-square-mile area. Could be Waymo’s first international market. Source: TechCrunch

    Amazon Bio Discovery

    Amazon Web Services launched Amazon Bio Discovery, an AI application for early-stage drug discovery allowing scientists to run complex computational workflows without writing code. Early adopters include Bayer, Broad Institute, and Voyager Therapeutics. Source: Reuters

    Google Chrome AI Skills

    Google added AI Skills to Chrome, allowing users to save and reuse AI prompts across different web pages. Features tie into Gemini integration with a library of common tasks. Source: TechCrunch
