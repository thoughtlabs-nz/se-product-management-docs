# Reporting Templates and Standards

## Research Output Types

### 1. Technology Brief
**Purpose**: Quick overview of an emerging technology or trend  
**Audience**: CTO, CEO  
**Length**: 1-2 pages  
**Frequency**: As discovered

**Template**:
```markdown
# Technology Brief: [Technology Name]

## Summary
[2-3 sentence executive summary]

## What It Is
[Brief technical explanation]

## Why It Matters
[Business relevance and opportunity]

## Disruption Potential
- Which business models could be disrupted?
- Which intermediaries could be displaced?
- What manual work could be automated?

## Current State
- **Maturity**: [Early/Growing/Mature]
- **Adoption**: [Examples of companies/products]
- **Momentum**: [Funding, hiring, launches]

## Recommendation
[Investigate further / Prototype / Monitor / Pass]

## References
[Key sources and links]
```

### 2. Deep Dive Research Report
**Purpose**: Comprehensive analysis of a technology or market opportunity  
**Audience**: Leadership team  
**Length**: 5-15 pages  
**Frequency**: Bi-weekly to monthly

**Template**:
```markdown
# Research Report: [Topic]

## Executive Summary
[1 paragraph overview with key findings and recommendation]

## Technology Overview
- Technical architecture and capabilities
- Key players and solutions
- Technical challenges and limitations

## Market Analysis
- Market size and growth projections
- Customer segments and use cases
- Competitive landscape
- Pricing and business models

## Disruption Analysis
### Business Model Disruption
[How this disrupts existing models]

### Intermediary Displacement
[What middlemen are affected]

### Automation Potential
[What manual work gets automated]

## Opportunity Assessment
- Strategic fit with company goals
- Technical feasibility
- Market timing
- Competitive advantages
- Resource requirements

## Risk Factors
- Technical risks
- Market risks
- Competitive risks
- Execution risks

## Financial Projections
- TAM/SAM/SOM analysis
- Unit economics
- Revenue projections
- Cost structure

## Recommendation
[Clear recommendation: Pursue/Prototype/Monitor/Pass]

## Next Steps
[Specific actions if recommendation is to proceed]

## Appendix
[Supporting data, charts, references]
```

### 3. Prototype Documentation
**Purpose**: Document a working proof-of-concept  
**Audience**: Technical team, CTO  
**Length**: 3-10 pages + code  
**Frequency**: Per prototype

**Template**:
```markdown
# Prototype: [Project Name]

## Overview
[What was built and why]

## Hypothesis
[What we were trying to validate]

## Technical Implementation
### Architecture
[System design and components]

### Technology Stack
[Languages, frameworks, services used]

### Key Features
[What it can do]

### Code Repository
[Link to GitHub/GitLab]

## Results
### What Worked
[Validated hypotheses]

### What Didn't Work
[Failed hypotheses and learnings]

### Metrics
[Performance, cost, user feedback]

## Business Implications
- Market opportunity validated/invalidated
- Technical feasibility confirmed/rejected
- Competitive positioning insights
- Go-to-market learnings

## Next Steps
### If Pursuing Further
[Path to MVP, company formation, etc.]

### If Not Pursuing
[Lessons learned, alternative approaches]

## Resources Required
- Engineering effort
- Infrastructure costs
- Additional research needed

## Appendix
[Screenshots, code samples, test results]
```

**Length**: Brief bullet points  
**Frequency**: Weekly

```markdown
- [Notable discoveries worth highlighting]
```

## Quality Standards

### Research Quality
- **Sources**: Cite all sources with links
- **Recency**: Prioritize recent data (< 6 months)
- **Verification**: Cross-reference multiple sources
- **Objectivity**: Acknowledge limitations and uncertainties
- **Clarity**: Write for executive audience, explain technical concepts

### Document Standards
- **Format**: Markdown (.md files)
- **Location**: Store in project folder
- **Naming**: Use descriptive names with dates (e.g., `2026-04-09_llm_agents_brief.md`)
- **Structure**: Follow templates for consistency
- **Links**: Preserve all reference links

### Visual Standards
- Use Mermaid diagrams for flows and architectures
- Include charts and graphs for data
- Screenshots for UI/UX concepts
- Keep visuals simple and clear

## Review Process

1. **Self-review**: Check for clarity, completeness, accuracy
2. **Peer review**: Get feedback from other researchers if available
3. **Stakeholder review**: Present to CTO for input
4. **Finalize**: Incorporate feedback and publish
5. **Archive**: Maintain organized library of research outputs

## Communication Guidelines

### When to Send Updates
- **Immediate**: Critical findings or opportunities
- **Weekly**: Regular status updates
- **Milestone**: Completion of major research or prototype
- **As Requested**: Ad-hoc questions or deep dives

### Communication Channels
- **Paperclip**: Task updates and formal deliverables
- **Slack**: Quick questions and informal updates
- **Email**: Formal reports to broader audience
- **Presentations**: Key decision points and major findings

### Presentation Best Practices
- Lead with the answer/recommendation
- Support with evidence and analysis
- Address risks and challenges upfront
- Be clear about confidence level
- Provide concrete next steps
