# Key Focus Areas and Technology Domains

## Strategic Themes

### 1. Business Model Disruption
Technologies and approaches that fundamentally change how value is created and captured:
- **Decentralization**: Blockchain, distributed systems, peer-to-peer networks
- **Platform Economics**: Multi-sided marketplaces, network effects, API-first
- **Subscription/Usage Models**: From ownership to access, consumption-based pricing
- **Zero Marginal Cost**: Software, digital goods, automated services

### 2. Intermediary Displacement
Technologies that eliminate middlemen and reduce friction:
- **Direct Channels**: Producer-to-consumer platforms
- **Smart Contracts**: Automated execution without intermediaries
- **Disintermediation**: Removing layers in supply chains
- **Self-Service Platforms**: Empowering end users to DIY

### 3. Human Automation at Scale
Technologies that augment or replace manual human work:
- **AI/ML**: Machine learning, deep learning, neural networks
- **Generative AI**: Large language models, code generation, content creation
- **RPA**: Robotic process automation, workflow automation
- **Autonomous Systems**: Self-driving, robotics, smart agents

## Evaluation Criteria

Each technology domain is evaluated on:
- **Disruption Potential**: Ability to transform existing business models
- **Technical Maturity**: Readiness for production deployment
- **Market Timing**: Current adoption curve and inflection points
- **Competitive Dynamics**: Incumbents, startups, and barriers to entry
- **Regulatory Environment**: Legal constraints and enablers
- **Talent Availability**: Ease of building teams with required expertise
